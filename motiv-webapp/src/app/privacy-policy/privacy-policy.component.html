5 Type of data collected

The application will collect the following data categories:

  • User identity: for example, name, email address
  • Individual characteristics: for example, age, city
  • Opinions and facts reported by the user in surveys
  • Searches made in a transport route planner
  • Points and rewards achieved by the user by fulfilling tasks given by the application
  • Activity data: mode of transport used in movements made by the user
  • Position data: coordinates of the User in space and time
  • Use of the Application: quantification of the utilization of different features of the Application

6 Purposes

Personal data is collected for the following purposes:

      • Contacting the User for matters related to the use of the application and the topic of travel time worthwhileness;
      • Validation of the conditions of eligibility of the User to the benefits offered by the Associated Parties;
      • Analysis of mobility related choices and opinions for both academic and business research purposes, generically with the ultimate purpose of improving mobility services and infrastructure;
      • Provision of statistical information to the User on his, and the community of Users of Woorti as an aggregate, use of transport modes and individual and collective impacts of such use;
      • Analysis of the use of the Application by Users for the purpose of improving the application.

7 Mechanism of automatic activity and position data collection

 

The collection of activity and position data is operated in the following way:

  • Activity data: when the device is in movement, the application infers the type of activity, in particular what is the mode of transport in case the User is moving
  • Position data: unless stated otherwise by the user, the application collects data about the geographical position on the user across time.

7 Data pseudonymization and exceptions

All data will be pseudoanonymized, by separating the storage of identity data from the user, from other data automatically or manually collected from the user.

The only exception to this rule is the data related to:

  • Points and rewards achieved by the user by fulfilling tasks given by the application

 

The reason for this exception is that it will be necessary both to confirm the user’s eligibility to prizes and the identity of the user for the purpose of contacting and delivering prizes.

8 Data recipients

 

The following categories of parties could be given access such data for the described purposes:

  • Local authorities or other third parties, for mobility infrastructure and service planning, policy evaluation or product research;
  • MoTiV project consortium members: the campaign behind the Application is at least partly being held under the European MoTiV project (http://motivproject.eu/). The Controller may share data to other parties for the purpose of the project research activities, framed within the list of purposes of data collection expressed above.
  • The duly pseudoanonymized data may be made open access.

 

The Controller will establish bilateral agreements with these parties to guarantee the conformity of the data use by the parties to the existing regulations.

9 Period

 

  1. The data will be stored until the purposes (point 5) of such storage persist. The persistence of the purposes in time depend on the following events:
      • The continuation of the Woorti campaign
      • The continuation of the User participation in the Woorti campaign

 


a  The continuation of the possibility for the movement positioning data to be useful for local mobility planning or historical statistical purposes

  1. Each individual data category will be subject to data minimization as soon as the purpose for which it was collected expires. For example, a User's identity data will be deleted when the User opts to cancel his participation.

 

10 Rights of the User

  1. Right of access by the data subject: The User has the right to obtain from the Controller confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning him or her are being processed, and, where that is the case, access to the personal data and information according to Art. 15 of the Regulation.
  2. Right to rectification: The User has the right to obtain from the Controller the rectification of inaccurate personal data concerning him or her.
  3. Right to erasure: The User has the right to obtain from the Controller the erasure of personal data concerning him or her where one of the grounds stated in Art. 17 of the Regulation apply.
  4. Right to restriction of processing: The User has the right to obtain from the controller restriction of processing.
  5. Right to data portability: The User has the right to receive the personal data concerning him or her, which he or she has provided to a controller, in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format and have the right to transmit those data to another controller.
  6. Right not to be subject to automated individual decision-making: the User has the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects concerning him or her or similarly significantly affects him or her.
